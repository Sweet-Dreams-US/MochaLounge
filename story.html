Our Story

A corner.
A coffee.
A community.

Twenty years of regulars, baristas, and the same 6:30am opening. This is the story of how a small Fort Wayne lounge grew up — without losing the corner booth.

Chapter 01

2004 —
Covington Road.

Brian Till opened the doors of Mocha Lounge on September 10, 2004 — a quiet corner of Covington Road, a small espresso machine, and a hunch that Fort Wayne wanted somewhere to slow down.

It wasn't a chain. It wasn't a drive-thru. It was a lounge in the truest sense of the word — couches, free Wi-Fi before that was a given, and a barista behind the counter who learned your name by your second visit. The pecan coffee cake was on the case from week one. It still is.

"We didn't want a coffee shop.
We wanted a living room
for the neighborhood."

— Brian Till, Founder
Chapter 02

The lounge
grew up.

After fourteen years on Covington and a steady rhythm of regulars, the team opened a second location on East Dupont in the late 2010s — the northside finally got its own corner booth.

Same espresso. Same recipes. Same baked-this-morning pastry case. Two corners of Fort Wayne, one lounge.

What stays the same

Twenty years on,
the bar's still high.

01 — Craft

Made-to-order, every order.

No pre-built sandwiches. No syrup pumped from a box. The slower way, on purpose.

02 — Local

Fort Wayne first.

Local produce where it's in season. Local relationships year-round. We grew up here.

03 — Welcome

A seat at the table.

Bring your laptop, your toddler, your book club. We've kept your booth.
